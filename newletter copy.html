💭 Top 5 DevOps Interview Questions (With Model Answers)

As the demand for DevOps-related roles increases, being well-prepared for your interview is crucial to stand out amongst applicants. 

Here are the top 5 frequently-asked DevOps interview questions that you can take notes of. 

#1 “Tell me about yourself”

In any Cloud interview, this question is your opportunity to provide a concise overview of your professional journey. 

This includes your experiences, skills, and your purpose to start a career or develop in the Cloud. 

However, it’s best to keep things short and memorable. 

I’d advice your to tailor your response to highlight relevant experiences and skills that showcase your fit for the role you're interviewing for.

#2 “Why did you choose our company?”

Your response to this question should demonstrate your genuine interest and understanding of the company's mission, values, and how your skills align with their objectives and/or current commitment.

It’s always good to be specific about which aspect(s) of the company's culture, products, or initiatives that resonate with you and how you see yourself contributing to its success.

This will show that you have done your research properly, which will create a great first impression. 

#3 “What innovation means to you and how have you applied it in your work?”

In the Cloud industry, innovation is a required trait for anyone involved to drive progress and development in the industry. 

This question tests your understanding of innovation and your ability to practice it.

That being said, you can provide examples of how you have included innovation in past roles, whether through implementing new technologies or finding innovative solutions to address challenges.

#4 “Tell me a time when you found your work challenging. How did you respond to it?”

As challenges are inevitable in any professional journey, you are asked to demonstrate your problem-solving abilities, resilience, and adaptability when faced with obstacles.

You can do this by focusing on the steps you took to overcome the challenge, including any strategies you implemented or support you sought to achieve a successful outcome.

Make sure to highlight your initiative and the impact you created afterwards. 

#5 “Walk me through an event when you reviewed a peer’s work and spotted an error. How did you communicate it to them?”

Effective communication and constructive feedback are essential to build effective collaboration, especially in the Cloud where accuracy is key. 

Showcasing your interpersonal skills when answering this question can help you stand out as a candidate.

By describing how you approached the situation with empathy and professionalism, your employer will see how you maintained a positive working relationship while addressing the error constructively.

🗣 Model Answers to DevOps Interview Questions

Now that you know the purpose of each question, here are the model answers to help you ace your upcoming interview.

#1 “Tell me about yourself”

Hi, I'm Lucy, a recent graduate with a degree in Computer Science, where I specialized in software architecture.

During my academic projects and internships, I focused on developing scalable solutions and understanding the fundamentals of cloud infrastructure, which sparked my interest in becoming a Solutions Architect. 

I have hands-on experience with AWS and Azure, gained through coursework and personal projects, including designing a cloud-based application that improved data processing times.

I'm passionate about leveraging technology to solve business challenges and am keen on applying my knowledge and fresh perspective to contribute to innovative projects. 

#2 “Why did you choose our company?”

I've been following Company X’s journey closely for quite some time now, and I'm particularly drawn to the way Company X prioritizes customer success and invests in cutting-edge technologies to deliver scalable and secure solutions. 

Your recent initiatives in machine learning and AI-driven cloud services align perfectly with my passion for leveraging technology to drive business outcomes. 

I'm eager to contribute my expertise in cloud architecture and DevOps practices to further Company X's mission of empowering small businesses through Cloud innovation.

#3 “What innovation means to you and how have you applied it in your work?”

Innovation to me is all about pushing boundaries and utilizing technologies to drive meaningful change.

In my previous role, I had the opportunity to lead an initiative to optimize Cloud infrastructure and enhance operational efficiency. 

The project’s end-goal was implementing a microservices architecture leveraging Kubernetes, which not only improved scalability and resilience but also reduced deployment times by 30%. 

Ever since then, I actively encouraged a culture of innovation within my team by fostering collaboration, experimenting with new tools and methodologies, and continuously seeking feedback to improve our processes.

After several trials-and-errors, we were able to reduce deployment times by 50%. 

#4 “Tell me a time when you found your work challenging. How did you respond to it?”

As an operations associate at Company Z, my team and I once encountered a significant challenge during a migration project to a Cloud-native environment.

Despite meticulous planning, we encountered unforeseen compatibility issues with legacy systems, causing delays and impacting project timelines. 

In response, I took a proactive approach by collaborating closely with the developers’ team to identify root causes and design several mitigation strategies.

This involved conducting thorough system assessments and prioritizing critical tasks to keep the project on track. 

Through collaboration and agile problem-solving, we were able to overcome the challenges, complete the migration, and document valuable insights from the issue as reference for future projects.

#5 “Walk me through an event when you reviewed a peer’s work and spotted an error. How did you communicate it to them?”

When I worked in a project management team, there was an instance where I reviewed a colleague's project plan and noticed a discrepancy in the timeline for completing a crucial milestone. 

Instead of pointing out the error on the spot, I scheduled a one-on-one meeting with him to discuss the project timeline, ensuring to highlight the positive aspects of their work first to maintain a supportive atmosphere. 

During our meeting, I raised my concerns about the timeline, emphasizing the importance of aligning it with the overall project schedule to ensure successful delivery. 

I provided examples from past projects and suggested adjustments to the timeline that would allow for better coordination with other team members and resources. 

I appreciated that my colleague was open to making the necessary adjustments, which ultimately contributed to the successful completion of the project within the agreed-upon timeline.
